- Original: "The paper finds that rollups are the most promising Layer 2 scaling approach for Ethereum, with optimistic rollups currently leading in adoption and general-purpose computation support, while ZK rollups are expected to dominate long-term due to faster finality (minutes vs. 7 days for withdrawals) and privacy advantages."
  Issue: The paper never claims rollups are "the most promising Layer 2 scaling approach." The paper's conclusion (Section VII) states: "This paper surveys all first and second layer blockchain scaling solutions. We give a brief explanation of every scaling solution and we give an in-depth explanation of rollups and their mode of operation." The paper surveys rollups but does not make a comparative ranking declaring them the most promising. Additionally, the paper says ZK rollups will "gain more in popularity" (citing Buterin), not "dominate."
  Changed to: "The paper surveys all first and second layer blockchain scaling solutions, with an in-depth focus on rollups and their mode of operation. Optimistic rollups currently lead in adoption and general-purpose computation support, while ZK rollups are expected to gain more popularity in the long term thanks to faster finality (minutes vs. 7 days for withdrawals) and privacy advantages."
