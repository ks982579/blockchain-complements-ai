## Overview

This paper proposes a Blockchain-based marketplace for trading and collaboratively improving AI/ML models using IoT-generated data, targeting the federated learning (FL) setting where "buyers" publish models needing training and "sellers" (IoT devices) contribute local computation on their private data in exchange for incentives. The work addresses two intertwined gaps in FL-based data/model markets: the lack of a trustworthy, decentralized infrastructure to coordinate model exchange and payment (avoiding the single point of failure and manipulation risk of a centralized aggregator/platform), and the lack of a fair, efficient mechanism to value each participant's data contribution for incentive payouts. The system is built on a permissionless Ethereum blockchain with smart contracts governing deposits, model transmission, training, aggregation, settlement, and token-based payment channels, with raw models/data stored off-chain via IPFS and only hashes recorded on-chain for scalability. Its core technical contribution is an Approximate Federated Shapley Value (AFS) algorithm—an engineered variant of Truncated Monte-Carlo Shapley value estimation tailored to FL—that reconstructs models from per-round gradient updates to estimate each seller's marginal contribution without the prohibitive cost of retraining on all 2^N data-source combinations, and the paper derives a Hoeffding-based performance bound relating sampling size to approximation error. Experiments on a prototype testbed (Ethereum/Ganache, IPFS, five Raspberry Pi devices, MNIST dataset across four data-distribution scenarios) show AFS achieves competitive or superior accuracy-vs-runtime trade-offs against exact Shapley, Single-Cal, and Multi-Cal baselines, the overall approach achieves competitive run-time performance with roughly a 15% drop in the cost of execution, and incentive allocations track each seller's data quantity (dataset size) fairly across scenarios, with data quality-based valuation explicitly left out of scope. Overall, the paper's contribution is an integrated, empirically validated ecosystem combining DLT-based trust/transparency with a computationally tractable, FL-adapted Shapley valuation scheme to enable fair, transparent ML model trading among heterogeneous IoT participants.
